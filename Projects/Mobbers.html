Mobbers

Mobbers is a roguelike dungeon shooter with randomised items, rooms, and enemies. Choose to play as the Goblin, Skeleton, Slime or Kobold and turn against the other mobs in the dungeon to escape the ever approaching heroes trying to loot your home.

Masters degree project turned into demo on Steam under the company Septagram Games.

I am the lead programmer and co-founder of Septagram Games. My core responsibilities on Mobbers is communication between the different disciplines, providing technical advice & support, and creating game functionality such as gameplay, UI/UX, and dungeon generation.

A level consists of multiple rooms that the players can tranverse between.

For the placement of rooms I use a grid and from the central point place a start room that only has a north entrance. From this initial room, potential rooms are branched off and checks whether there is already a room there. This continues more branches are created either a desired number of rooms or created or there has been enough attempts to place a room.

Once a layout is chosen, neigbouring rooms would be counted and the room would be assigned its room oriantation with doors representing a direction on a compass (NESW).

With the room oriantations, rooms can be selected from a folder labelled with the appropriate directions, with these rooms being stored as 64x64 files team members can make with colours representing what type of object is placed, such as black and greys used for walls and corners, blues used for flooring, and red for traps.

When generating a room, the generator stores all the pixels and checks each one to a corresponding colour in a dictionary and spawns a prefab of that type, if it is an obstacle then a random one is chosen.

Once the prefab is spawned, the generator checks neighbouring objects and rotates appropriately.

Textures are chosen based on the pretermined theme. All available textures for a theme are stored on texture atlases which is zoomed in and uses random offsets to create unique looking room sections without require lots of textures. Themes available are:

  • Kingly Castle
  • Dark Dungeon
  • Ludacris Library
  • Desolate Desert

Along with folders for different room oriantations, special rooms can be placed which allow special requirements, these rooms include:

  • Start room with only north doors
  • Miniboss rooms that are open spaces with minimal obstacles
  • Shop rooms with only a northern door to boss room and shop stands
  • Boss rooms that are very open and have south and north doors

Once the layout of the dungeon and rooms are chosen, the dungeon manager controls when rooms are created and players are moved between rooms.

When players are spawned into a room, the neigbouring rooms are generated with the presets already chosen, reducing wait time from generating up to 50 rooms when starting to only generating a maximum of 3 during transition fades. Rooms the player aren't currently in are set inactive.

To help prevent the player from getting lost in the dungeon, a minimap is available that reveals explored rooms. This map uses the template provided for the room generation and only applies colours for the floors and walls.

Along with randomly room generation, a seed generator is available in editor to allow control of what is generated, allowing ease of testing for bugs by saving the seed and creating specific levels for marketing and playtests. Users can use both numbers and words which converts to hashcodes for seed assigning.

I created the UI functionality with both consoles and PC in mind, so the UI can be controlled by controllers, mouse, and keyboard with the latest used device taking control.

When starting the game, a cutscene plays while the main menu loads along with preparing shaders in advance. The user can watch the whole cutscene or after everything is ready can skip by pressing spacebar on a keyboard, leading to the menu.

The main menu consists of animated background panels that are floating vertically, Play game buttons, settings and the exit.

The main menu consists of animated background panels that are floating vertically, Play game buttons, settings and the exit.

The settings menu (found both in the main and pause menus) contains a fullscreen button, allowing to swap between that and window mode, a back button and volume settings.

The volume you are able to change all volume with 6 levels. You can also the music and game sound effects independently to each other, allowing the player to change a ratio of music to game sounds. The individual volumes are capped to the overall volume.

When leaving the settings they are immedietely saved to player prefs for future sessions.

The game can be paused once in a dungeon where it will remove all other UI elements for a fullscreen pause screen with the options for:

  • Resuming the game
  • Resetting the dungeon with a brand new seed
  • settings (same as the main menu)
  • Exit to main menu

After leaving the starting room in a dungeon. A timer will start that will countdown towards the boss fight, having the players to clear dungeon rooms and collect as much loot and coins as possible to prepare for the inevitable.

When entering a new room, the doors are locked behind the players until all the enemies within the room are all defeated.

Once a room is cleared, the doors are opened and a friendly mimic chest will spawn. After interacting with the mimi, the timer pauses offering a breather to the players to collect any drops and explore further. Once the players enter a new room, including rooms previously explored, the timer starts again until times up or the next chest.
