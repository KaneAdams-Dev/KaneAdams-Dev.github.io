Mobbers

Mobbers is a roguelike dungeon shooter with randomised items, rooms, and enemies. Choose to play as the Goblin, Skeleton, Slime or Kobold and turn against the other mobs in the dungeon to escape the ever approaching heroes trying to loot your home.

Masters degree project turned into demo on Steam under the company Septagram Games.

I am the lead programmer and co-founder of Septagram Games. My core responsibilities on Mobbers is communication between the different disciplines, providing technical advice & support, and creating game functionality such as gameplay, UI/UX, and dungeon generation.

A level consists of multiple rooms that the players can tranverse between.

For the placement of rooms I use a grid and from the central point place a start room that only has a north entrance. From this initial room, potential rooms are branched off and checks whether there is already a room there. This continues more branches are created either a desired number of rooms or created or there has been enough attempts to place a room.

Once a layout is chosen, neigbouring rooms would be counted and the room would be assigned its room oriantation with doors representing a direction on a compass (NESW).

With the room oriantations, rooms can be selected from a folder labelled with the appropriate directions, with these rooms being stored as 64x64 files team members can make with colours representing what type of object is placed, such as black and greys used for walls and corners, blues used for flooring, and red for traps.

When generating a room, the generator stores all the pixels and checks each one to a corresponding colour in a dictionary and spawns a prefab of that type, if it is an obstacle then a random one is chosen.

Once the prefab is spawned, the generator checks neighbouring objects and rotates appropriately.

Textures are chosen based on the pretermined theme. All available textures for a theme are stored on texture atlases which is zoomed in and uses random offsets to create unique looking room sections without require lots of textures.
